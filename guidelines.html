Community Guidelines

Last Updated: April 2026

YOR is a space where people share how they feel and connect through their moods. To keep it safe and welcoming for everyone, we ask that you follow these guidelines.

Profile Photos

Your profile photo is how others see you in YOR. To keep the experience comfortable for everyone, please make sure your photo:

Is appropriate. No nudity, sexually explicit or suggestive imagery. This includes photos that are partially obscured, filtered, or stylized to depict explicit content.

Is non-violent. No depictions of violence, gore, weapons, or anything intended to shock or disturb.

Is yours. Use a photo that represents you. Don't impersonate other people or use someone else's likeness without their permission.

Is not harmful. No hate symbols, slurs, or imagery that promotes discrimination based on race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, religion, or disability.

Is not misleading. No spam, scams, or deceptive imagery designed to trick other users.

We use a combination of automated tools and human review to check profile photos. Most photos are reviewed instantly. In some cases, our team may need a bit more time — we aim to review flagged content within 24 hours.

Reporting

If you see a profile photo that doesn't follow these guidelines, you can report it directly from the app. Tap the ··· menu next to the person's name and choose Report.

All reports are anonymous. The person you report will never know it was you.

Once we receive a report, our moderation team will review it and take action if needed. If multiple people report the same photo, it may be temporarily hidden until we make a decision.

What Happens if Your Photo Is Removed

If your photo doesn't meet our guidelines, here's what to expect:

First time. We'll replace your photo with a default avatar and send you a notification. You can upload a new photo right away.

Second time. You'll receive a warning that further violations may result in restrictions.

Third time. Photo uploads will be temporarily restricted for 7 days. After that, you can upload again.

Repeated violations. Photo uploads may be permanently restricted. You can contact our support team to discuss your account.

We don't share the specific reason a photo was removed. This is to prevent misuse of our moderation system.

Appeals

If you believe your photo was removed by mistake, please reach out to our support team. We're happy to take another look.

A Note on Kindness

YOR is built around moods, emotions, and honest connection. We believe that starts with treating each other with respect — even in the small things, like a profile photo. Thank you for helping us keep this space safe.

These guidelines may be updated from time to time. Last updated: April 2026.
